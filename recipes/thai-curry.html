What is Thai Curry?

Frankly, Thai Curry is a set of spices mixed together in a sauce or a paste combined with vegetables, a protein source such as beef or chicken, and is usually served with rice. The result of this combination is a dish bursting with diverse flavors.

The greatest part about Thai curry is the endless customization. You aren’t stuck to one way of making it, you can choose just the right balance of heat, salt, sour and sweet for your pallet t o enjoy.

Thai curry is not only a delicious cuisine but is one that tells a story of the land where it comes from. When traveling to various parts of the country you’ll find that the curries will be different. Curry from the south parts of Thailand will have a more rich numeric and peppercorn flavors found within them since they are grown easily in those areas. However, curry from Bangkok will have almost no turmeric in the flavor profile. In Bangkok they use more red and dry chilies along with galangal.

In short, Thai curries reflect the richness and agricultural diversity found within each of its regions.

Ingredients for Thai Curry

  • Chicken breast or chicken thigh, chefs choice.
  • Red Curry paste
  • Coconut milk
  • Bell pepper
  • Leek
  • Ginger and Garlic
  • Cilantro
  • Rice

Steps:

  1. Season the chicken and then sear on both sides until golden or desired temperature.
  2. Saute all vegetables and spices then stir in curry paste and let it bloom.
  3. Once the curry paste is mixed in, pour the coconut milk into the pan.
  4. Return the chicken to the skillet and then bake the Thai curry at 375 degrees farenheit.
  5. Let set, and then serve with rice or choice of starch.
